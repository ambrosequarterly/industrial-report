Key Themes Q2 2026:
• Demand broadened. Chicago posted its highest net absorption since 2023 (6.0M SF); Phoenix (4.7M SF), Indianapolis (3.1M SF), Cincinnati (2.7M SF), Columbus (1.9M SF), and Nashville (1.8M SF) all delivered strong quarters.
• Vacancy compression continued in Indianapolis (5.9%, lows not seen since 2022), Phoenix (9.6%, −70 bps QoQ), Cincinnati (4.0%, −60 bps QoQ), and Columbus (5.2%, −270 bps YoY).
• Pipelines are rebuilding selectively. Phoenix (18.4M SF, +31% QoQ), Chicago (11.8M SF), Columbus (9.0M SF), Nashville (8.6M SF), and Indianapolis (6.6M SF, all-speculative starts) are adding supply; Cleveland (324K SF) and Cincinnati (583K SF) remain supply-constrained.
• Soft spots: Cleveland posted negative absorption (−166K SF); Denver absorption moderated to 55K SF with direct vacancy up 30 bps to 8.3%; Orlando vacancy edged up to 10.2%.
• Macro (per CBRE, all Q2 reports): GDP growth projected at 2.2% with AI-related investment a key driver. The U.S./Iran conflict pushed inflation to 4.2%; a June peace deal, if realized, would bring inflation to the upper 3% range by year-end. Treasury yields above 4% continue to complicate real estate pricing.
• Cap rate data on each market page remains sourced from the CBRE H2 2025 Cap Rate Survey — the most recent published edition.

  • One summary email is sent listing which markets have new reports available and which are still pending

Embedded Reports

  • PDF filenames follow the pattern reports/[market]-q2-2026.pdf (e.g. reports/chicago-q2-2026.pdf)
